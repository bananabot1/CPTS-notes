**Overview:**
- POP3 is a simpler alternative to IMAP. It only supports listing, retrieving, and deleting emails. No folder structures, no server-side management, no multi-client synchronization.
- Operates on port 110 by default. Encrypted sessions use SSL/TLS on port 995. Without encryption, all commands, credentials, and email content are transmitted in plaintext.
---
## Dangerous Settings

|Setting|Description|
|---|---|
|`auth_debug`|Enables full authentication debug logging|
|`auth_debug_passwords`|Logs submitted passwords and their hashing scheme|
|`auth_verbose`|Logs failed authentication attempts and reasons|
|`auth_verbose_passwords`|Logs passwords used during authentication, optionally truncated|
|`auth_anonymous_username`|Sets the username for ANONYMOUS SASL mechanism logins|

---
## Enumeration

```
openssl s_client -connect <target>:995
```

Open a raw SSL connection to the POP3 service.

---
## POP3 Commands

|Command|Description|
|---|---|
|`USER <username>`|Identify the user to authenticate|
|`PASS <password>`|Authenticate with the user's password|
|`STAT`|Request the number of emails stored on the server|
|`LIST`|List all emails with their IDs and sizes|
|`RETR <id>`|Retrieve a specific email by ID|
|`DELE <id>`|Mark a specific email for deletion by ID|
|`CAPA`|Request the list of server capabilities|
|`RSET`|Reset the session, undoing any pending deletions|
|`QUIT`|Commit deletions and close the connection|
